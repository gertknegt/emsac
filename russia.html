Admiral Makarov State University of Maritime and Inland Shipping

The scenic beauty of the Netherlands has always instilled in me a desire to one day visit there. The Netherlands has been recognized as the knowledge center of long study traditions and well-known universities. Due to its central position, the Netherlands has been described as the Gateway to Europe and its key position in the new European Union and multinational market.

As a student of Marine engineering from the Admiral Makarov State University of Maritime and Inland Shipping (AMSUMIS), the EMSAC ‘13, I believe would be the opportunity of a lifetime for me and my colleagues to thoroughly contemplate the real width and depth of our field. Netherlands, having centuries old heritage of maritime operations, would definitely be the apt place to understand and appreciate the age old and perfected techniques in marine engineering.

Going through the programme brochure of EMSAC ’13, we are extremely excited and looking forward to the lineup of brilliantly molded events and titillating activities that will more than inspire us and further propel us in our career endeavors. Moreover, the exposure we get from the various interactions during the conference with students and faculty from all over Europe, would I believe, help in our intellectual and cognitive development. Keeping apart the academic side, we sincerely hope to forge strong bonds of friendship with our fellow attendees and we’re really looking forward to the various social events that the EMSAC is organizing.
